# Clinic Lead Leak Checklist

## How to Use It

- Score each item `0`, `1`, or `2`.
- `0` = not in place
- `1` = partly in place
- `2` = working consistently

## Section 1: First Response Speed

1. New leads are answered within 5 minutes.
2. Out-of-hours leads are handled first thing the next morning.
3. Phone, form, and WhatsApp leads are visible in one place.
4. Missed calls are called back the same day.
5. The team uses a standard first-response message.
6. Response speed is tracked by team member.

## Section 2: WhatsApp Quality

7. First messages move the conversation forward, not just answer questions.
8. There is a ready-made response flow for price enquiries.
9. The clinic has trust-building replies for hesitant leads.
10. Team members write in a consistent tone.
11. Messages include a clear next step.
12. Stalled conversations are spotted and reviewed.

## Section 3: Follow-Up Discipline

13. There is a follow-up schedule for silent leads.
14. Leads receive at least 2-3 planned touches after the first exchange.
15. Follow-up depends on process, not memory.
16. The team knows who needs another contact attempt each day.
17. Leads are not abandoned before a final reactivation attempt.
18. Follow-up outcomes are recorded.

## Section 4: Appointments and No-Shows

19. Patients receive an immediate confirmation after booking.
20. A reminder goes out 48 hours before the appointment.
21. A final confirmation happens 24 hours before the slot.
22. No-show patients receive a rebooking message.
23. The clinic measures no-show rate.
24. The team reviews no-show reasons regularly.

## Section 5: Old Lead Data

25. The clinic still has the last 6-12 months of old leads.
26. Old leads are segmented in a useful way.
27. There are ready-made reactivation messages.
28. Old leads are revisited on a planned basis.
29. Responses from old leads are reclassified properly.
30. Old data is reviewed before spending more on acquisition.
