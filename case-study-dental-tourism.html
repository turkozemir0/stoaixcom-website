1Problem

Nine-hour delays in a market where minutes decide.

Dental tourism from the UK, Germany, and the Gulf to Turkey has grown significantly over the last five years. Patients travel for smile design, veneers, and implants — treatments that cost two to four times more at home. The Ankara clinic at the centre of this case study had invested in high-quality equipment, English and Arabic-speaking staff, and strong patient photography to build an international reputation.

Their lead generation was working. Enquiries arrived daily from Instagram, their website, and WhatsApp — mostly from the UK and Germany. What wasn't working was their follow-up. The patient coordinator responsible for international leads was handling her own clinical admin alongside enquiry management. Average response time to new international leads: 9.4 hours. On Fridays and weekends, leads often waited 36 hours or more.

International dental patients are comparison shopping. They submit enquiries to three, four, sometimes five clinics simultaneously — and they book with whoever responds first, explains the process most clearly, and instils the most confidence. A 9-hour delay didn't just lose leads to speed; it damaged perceived professionalism before the patient had even received a quote.

The clinic also held 6,800 past enquiries — people who had asked about treatment costs, requested photos, or started a consultation process but never confirmed. Many of these had simply gone cold due to a lack of timely follow-up. No one had the capacity to go back through them.

2Solution

Multilingual AI. Instant replies. Systematic reactivation.

STOAIX deployed a multilingual AI agent across the clinic's WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, and website chat — configured to respond in English, German, and Arabic within 3 minutes of an enquiry being received, regardless of the time of day or day of the week.

The AI was trained on the clinic's treatment catalogue, pricing structure, before-and-after results, and the logistics of international patient coordination — flight timing, accommodation options, treatment duration, and post-procedure care. It could answer the full range of questions an international patient asks before committing to travel, without human intervention.

Qualified leads — those who had confirmed a realistic timeline and expressed clear treatment intent — were flagged for the patient coordinator to handle a video consultation. The AI managed all pre-qualification: budget range, treatment type, preferred dates, any previous dental work. By the time the coordinator joined a conversation, the patient was already engaged and informed.

The 6,800 archived contacts were segmented by treatment interest and date of last contact, then reactivated through a phased outreach campaign. Contacts from the last 6 months received a personalised follow-up message. Older contacts received a reintroduction framed around new availability and a treatment showcase. The AI handled all responses, escalating only when a contact was ready to confirm.

"Our coordinators were spending 70% of their time on enquiries that never converted — qualifying, chasing, translating. Now the AI handles all of that. Our team only gets involved when a patient is genuinely ready to book. Our conversion rate has nearly doubled."

Patient Services Director
Dental Tourism Clinic, Ankara
3Result

+62% international bookings. 6,800 cold leads reactivated.

Within 8 weeks of deployment, international consultation bookings increased by 62%. The primary driver was response speed: being first to reply — in the patient's own language — created a trust advantage that the clinic had previously been giving away through delays.

The reactivation of 6,800 archived contacts produced 204 confirmed consultations — a 3% conversion rate from leads that had previously been abandoned. For smile design packages averaging £1,800 and implant treatments ranging from £800 to £2,400, the return from the reactivation campaign alone covered the cost of STOAIX many times over.

The patient coordination team reported a 71% reduction in the time spent on initial enquiry management. Rather than processing inbound messages across multiple languages for the majority of their working day, coordinators now focus exclusively on confirmed consultation calls and in-person patient coordination. The quality of each consultation improved because coordinators were no longer arriving exhausted from an inbox backlog.

The clinic has since expanded STOAIX to handle post-treatment follow-up — checking in with patients after procedures, collecting reviews, and prompting return visits. What began as an enquiry management solution has become the backbone of the clinic's entire patient communication infrastructure.
